Section 02

Pre-Execution Readiness & Worker Welfare

Before commencing construction, establishing proper welfare facilities for workers is not only a legal requirement but also a key indicator of ethical and responsible practice.

💧

Clean Water Supply

Provision of potable drinking water at all worker-accessible locations across the site at all times.

🏠

Rest & Shelter

Adequate shelters, eating areas, and accommodation where applicable per BOCW standards.

🩺

Medical Support

First aid facilities and emergency medical response protocols on-site with trained personnel.

🚿

Sanitation

Hygienic toilet and wash facilities maintained to health standards, with separate amenities for women.

Section 03

Environmental & Sustainability Compliance

Modern construction must adopt a sustainability-first approach — minimizing environmental impact while optimizing resource efficiency.

Sewage Treatment Plants (STP) must be installed for sites exceeding 20,000 sqm. Water recycling for construction use (curing, dust suppression) is mandatory. Controlled sourcing via tanker logs prevents groundwater depletion. All wastewater discharge points must be monitored with pH strips daily.

STP Installation Rainwater Harvesting Pits Water Metering Zero Discharge Policy

Delhi NCR GRAP norms require strict dust control. Anti-smog guns must operate during peak activity hours. All bare soil areas must be covered with hessian cloth or jute nets. Vehicle wheel wash units are compulsory at exit gates.

Anti-Smog Guns Dust Separators Water Sprinkling (3x daily) Wheel Wash Facility Material Covering (tarpaulin) Green Net Hoarding

Solar-powered temporary lighting systems significantly reduce dependency on diesel generators, cutting both cost and emissions. LED lighting across site hoardings and worker areas is standard practice. DG sets must comply with CPCB norms and run on low-sulphur diesel.

Solar Temporary Lighting LED Floodlights CPCB-Compliant DG Sets Energy Audit Quarterly

Noisy operations (breaking, piling, crushing) are restricted to 6AM–10PM as per CPCB guidelines. Noise monitoring at site boundary must not exceed 75 dB(A) during daytime. Sound barriers should surround high-noise equipment.

Noise Monitoring Log Sound Barriers Time-Restricted Blasting Community Notification

Section 05

Waste Management & Resource Optimization

Effective waste management is a cornerstone of sustainable construction. All waste streams must be identified, tracked, and responsibly managed.

🧱

C&D Waste

Construction and demolition debris segregated, recycled, or disposed via authorized vendors per MoEF C&D Waste Rules 2016.

🔩

Steel Scrap

Scrap metal collected daily and directed to licensed recyclers. Maintain consignment notes and weight records.

🪵

Wooden Waste

Offcuts and formwork timber reused where possible; remainder disposed responsibly. No site burning permitted.

🛢️

Hazardous Waste

Used oil, paint waste & chemical containers stored in bunded areas and handed over to CPCB-authorised recyclers.

A complete waste register must be maintained and available for inspection at all times. Each category requires a dedicated segregated storage zone with signage.

Section 06

Fire Safety & Hazard Management

Fire safety during construction is often overlooked despite the presence of flammable materials and temporary electrical setups. GRAP norm compliance is essential in Delhi NCR during high-pollution periods.

Extinguishers placed at every 15m interval, welding bays, electrical DB boards, and chemical stores. Monthly inspection and pressure checks mandatory.

Extinguisher Grid (every 15m) Fire Response Drills (quarterly) Safe Chemical Storage Hot Work Permit System GRAP Compliance Electrical Audit (monthly)

A site-specific Emergency Response Plan (ERP) must be prepared, communicated to all workers during induction, and displayed at site entrance and every floor. Assembly point must be marked and unobstructed at all times.

Section 07

Chemical & Hazardous Material Management

Handling chemicals safely is crucial to prevent environmental contamination and health hazards across the site and surrounding areas.

Every chemical on-site must have a current MSDS sheet stored at the store and available to workers in their language. GHS hazard labels must be affixed on all containers.

MSDS Binder at Store GHS Labelling Secondary Containment (110% volume) Spill Kits at Chemical Stores Incompatibility Segregation Safe Handling Training
Section 08

Soil Conservation & Natural Resource Protection

Construction activities often disturb natural soil conditions. Preserving soil integrity and preventing erosion are essential environmental obligations.

Silt fences and sediment traps must be installed along drainage pathways. Excavated topsoil must be stored separately and reused in landscaping to preserve fertility.

Controlled Excavation Retaining Structures Silt Fences at Boundary Topsoil Stockpile Management Excavated Soil Reuse Plan
Section 09

ESG Compliance & Carbon Footprint Reduction

ESG principles are becoming increasingly central to construction project evaluation, financing, and social license to operate.

🌿

Efficient Materials

Precise material planning and green alternatives (AAC blocks, fly-ash bricks) to reduce embodied carbon.

⚙️

Low-Emission Machinery

Preference for BS-VI compliant or electric plant and equipment on-site wherever feasible.

🚛

Transport Optimization

Logistics planning to minimize vehicle trips, using GPS-tracked fleets with load efficiency targets.

📊

Carbon Accounting

Monthly GHG inventory maintained across Scope 1 (DG sets, vehicles) and Scope 2 (electricity) emissions.

IGBC Green Homes / Green Factory is the most common Indian certification. GRIHA is mandatory for government projects above 500 sqm. LEED India is preferred by multinational occupiers. EHS controls during construction directly contribute to construction activity credits in all three rating systems.

IGBC Green Homes GRIHA (Mandatory Gov't) LEED India BEE Star Rating WELL Building Standard

Section 11

Awareness & Continuous Improvement

Maintaining high EHS standards requires a culture of continuous learning, not just periodic compliance checks.

Daily Toolbox Talks (TBT) — 10-minute briefings each morning covering the day's specific hazards, any near-miss learnings from the previous day, PPE checks, and weather advisories. Attendance must be recorded and signed.

Toolbox Talks (TBT) PPE Inspection Site Hazard Walk Near-Miss Reporting

Weekly Safety Committee Meeting — EHS officer, supervisors, and sub-contractor leads review the week's incidents, unsafe acts observed, inspection findings, and upcoming high-risk activities. Action items tracked to closure.

Safety Committee Meeting Sub-contractor Review Site Safety Inspection Action Closure Tracking

Monthly EHS Audit — Structured inspection against a checklist covering all site areas, documentation review, and worker interviews. Results reported to Project Director with a corrective action plan. Leading & lagging indicators published.

Formal EHS Audit Corrective Action Plan KPI Dashboard Update Feedback Mechanisms

Annual Management Review — Comprehensive EHS performance review by senior management covering incident trends, regulatory compliance status, audit findings, training records, and targets for the next year. Feeds into ESG reporting.

Management Review Meeting Annual EHS Report ESG Disclosure Update Target Setting for Next Year
